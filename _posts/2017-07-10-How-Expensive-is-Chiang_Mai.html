Chiang Mai, in the north of Thailand, has a growing reputation for being the world’s best city for location independent workers. It offers comfortable accommodation, amazing cuisine, and endless entertainment all for an amazingly low price. But just how much can you expect to spend if you choose to make Chiang Mai your home on the road?

I’ve recorded my spending habits for an entire month of working in and travelling around Chiang Mai. On average, I spent $27 a day, or just under $800 for the entire month of June.

A budget of around $30 a day allows for a lifestyle that would cost at least three times as much in the west. I didn’t cook a single meal, spending between $1 and $5 to eat out at local or western restaurants. The $220 apartment rental was perfectly located, spacious, and included a weekly deep cleaning service. Dirty laundry was promptly washed, ironed, folded and delivered to my door for under $3 a week.

On top of essential purchases, $30 a day offers an extensive entertainment budget. I travelled over 1000 kilometers on a scooter rental that cost under $100, gas and all, for the entire month. I visited an elephant sanctuary for $45. Even a ticket to the cinema only costs $5. There are endless temples, waterfalls, caves, and mountains to be explored in and around Chiang Mai for no cost at all.

Of course, you could spend more than $800 a month if you tried. Upgrading a scooter to a motorcycle would at least double the rental cost. The fanciest apartments will set you back $600 instead of $220. Imported beer and cheese can also be very expensive. The sky is the limit, just like anywhere else. The point is, it is extremely easy to live and travel comfortably on $800 a month. Chris over at christhefreelancer.com wrote a detailed post on living in Chiang Mai for under $600 a month.

Conclusions

As you can see, I didn’t cut corners to make this budget work - it was all pretty natural. I was even able to (over) indulge in my relatively expensive coffee addiction!

So, if you’re interested in travelling to an amazing destination that won’t break the bank, Chiang Mai could be the place for you. If you’re interested in starting an online business and want to stretch your dollar and chances of success, Chiang Mai could be the place for you. If you’re passionate about pad thai and living cheaply, well… you get the point.
