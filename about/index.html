Wayfaring Worker

Hello! Welcome to Wayfaring Worker - I'm Fisher and I'm working and wandering my way all over the world.

I created Wayfaring Worker as a resource to record and share what I'm learning on this journey. My goal is to create a comprehensive resource to guide and inspire you to live a life more adventurous.

Within the pages of this website you'll find inspiring travel stories and photography, guides on pursuing an enjoyable career abroad, tips on location independent living, and so much more. These are the lessons I'm learning as I try to create my own fulfilling lifestyle - professionally and personally.

It all started with the father-son trip of a lifetime to beautiful Scotland. It didn't take me long to realize that I loved travelling, and that I was willing to do whatever it took to keep the adventure going.

It was the summer of 2016 when we took that trip to Scotland. I had just graduated college with my bachelor's degree in Geophysics. It was a celebratory trip that ended up bringing real conflict to my life. The trip was beyond amazing, like nothing I had ever experienced. I wanted more - but that wasn't the plan. I was supposed to work in my field, go to graduate school and earn six figures in the oil industry. That's what I had been preparing for after all. Was I going to let my efforts go to waste? I decided to try and sweep it all under the rug, accepting a job at the United States Geological Survey upon returning from Scotland.

At first, the work was enjoyable. It was nice to finally earn money from my field - no homework or exams to stress over... it was a welcome change. I even managed to negotiate five weeks of leave for another trip to Europe upon accepting the job offer, hoping that maybe this would be enough to make me happy.

A beautiful selfie of my friend Caroline and I as we frolic through the gardens at the Palace of Versailles near Paris, France.

Backpacking around Europe during the fall of 2016 solidified it for me: splurge trips just weren't enough. Expensive vacations that end too soon weren't my thing. I wanted to take my time and enjoy working, living, and exploring wherever I please. So, while I finished my obligations at the United States Geological Survey, my priorities shifted in a big way. I had to build a life of location independence that properly balances adventure and achievement.
