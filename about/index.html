Wayfaring Worker

Hello! Welcome to Wayfaring Worker - I'm Fisher and I'm working and wandering my way all over the world.

I created Wayfaring Worker as a resource to record and share what I'm learning along this journey. My goal is to create a great resource to guide and inspire you to live a life more adventurous.

Within the pages of this website you'll find inspiring travel stories and photography, guides on pursueing an enjoyable career abroad, tips on location independent living, and so much more. These are the lessons I'm learning as I try to create personally and professionally fulfilling lifestyle.

It all started with the father-son trip of a lifetime to beautiful Scotland. It didn't take me long to realize that I loved travelling, and that I was willing to do whatever it took to continue adventuring.

It was the summer of 2016 when we took that trip to Scotland. I had just graduated college with my bachelors degree in geophysics. It was a celebratory trip but travelling to Scotland brought real conflict to my life. The trip was beyond amazing, like nothing I had ever experienced. I wanted more, but that wasn't the plan. I was supposed to work in my field, go to graduate school and earn six-figures in the oil industry. That's what I had been preparing for after all, was I going to let my efforts go to waste? I decided to try and sweep my urge to travel under the rug, I accepted a job at the United States Geological Survey upon returning from Scotland.

At first the work was good, it was nice to finally earn money from my work. No homework or exams to stress over, it was a welcome change. I even managed to negotiate five weeks of leave for another trip to Europe upon accepting the job offer; I thought maybe this would be enough to make me happy.

A beautiful selfie of my friend Caroline and I as we frolic through the gardens at the Palace of Versailles near Paris, France.

Backpacking around Europe during the fall of 2016 solidified it to me - splurge trips just weren't enough. I didn't want quick and expensive vacations! I wanted to take my time and enjoy working, living, and exploring wherever I please. So while I finished my obligations at the United States Geological Survey, but I knew where my real priorities where. I had to build a life of location independence that properly balances adventure and achievement.
