Chiang Mai is known the world over as a digital nomad’s paradise. Comfortable living, delicious food, and endless entertainment are all at your fingertips for incredibly low prices. But just how low are they? Over the course of the month, I’ve recorded just how much you can expect to spend during an extended stay in Chiang Mai - the number might suprise you.

On average, I spent just under $27 per day, that's only $800 for the entire month.

$800 is what it cost me to work in, and travel around northern Thailand in great comfort. Of course you could spend much more than this, it all depends on how much luxury and travelling you'd like to indulge in.

It's absolutely possible to spend much less than $800 a month. Chris over at christhefreelancer.com provides a detailed account on living in Chiang Mai for under $600 a month.

My goal was not to work in the city for as cheap as possible. I used the city as a home base to explore the north. It was very tourism focused and experience / entertainment heavy month. Some of the major purchases in the name of tourism were a month long scooter rental, day trip to an elephant sanctuary, a week long trip around Mae Hong Son, night spent out at the muay thai fights, souvenir shopping, waterfalls, etc.
